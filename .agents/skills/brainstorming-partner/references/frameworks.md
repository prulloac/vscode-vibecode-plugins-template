# Brainstorming Frameworks

This reference documents the six frameworks used by the brainstorming-partner skill for idea generation.

## 1. SCAMPER

SCAMPER is a checklist-based method that systematically applies seven techniques to existing products, services, or processes.

### Framework

- **S**ubstitute: What components, materials, or attributes could be replaced?
- **C**ombine: What could be merged with other ideas or products?
- **A**dapt: What could be adjusted to serve other purposes?
- **M**odify: What could be magnified, minimized, or changed in form?
- **P**ut to other uses: How could it be repurposed or used differently?
- **E**liminate: What could be removed or simplified?
- **R**everse: What could be done in reverse order or inverted?

### Best For

Product improvements, process optimization, service redesign, features addition.

### Execution Pattern

For each letter, generate 3-4 specific ideas by asking targeted questions about the context.

## 2. Six Thinking Hats

Six Thinking Hats uses colored "hats" to represent different thinking modes, allowing parallel thinking from multiple perspectives.

### Framework

- **White Hat**: Facts, data, information (what do we know?)
- **Red Hat**: Emotions, intuition, gut feelings (what do we feel?)
- **Black Hat**: Critical thinking, risks, problems (what could go wrong?)
- **Yellow Hat**: Optimism, benefits, best case scenarios (what's the upside?)
- **Green Hat**: Creativity, alternatives, new ideas (what else could we try?)
- **Blue Hat**: Process control, organization, summaries (how do we proceed?)

### Best For

Complex problems needing diverse perspectives, team decision-making, evaluating proposals fairly.

### Execution Pattern

For each hat color, generate 2-3 ideas or perspectives specific to that thinking mode.

## 3. Mind Mapping

Mind Mapping creates a hierarchical visual structure of ideas radiating from a central theme, showing relationships and hierarchies.

### Framework

- Central Node: Core problem/topic
- Primary Branches: Main categories or aspects
- Secondary Branches: Sub-ideas and details
- Connections: Links showing relationships

### Best For

Breaking down complex topics, exploring relationships between ideas, visual organization, identifying gaps.

### Execution Pattern

Start with central theme. Create 4-6 main branches for key aspects. Generate 2-3 sub-ideas per branch.

## 4. Lateral Thinking / Random Word Association

Lateral thinking uses random stimuli to break habitual thinking patterns and create unexpected connections.

### Framework

- Select a random word unrelated to the problem
- Force connections between the word and the problem
- Explore unexpected associations and combinations
- Follow tangential paths to novel solutions

### Best For

Breaking through mental blocks, generating truly novel ideas, creative problem-solving.

### Execution Pattern

Generate 4-5 random, unrelated concepts. For each, force 1-2 creative connections to the brainstorming topic.

## 5. Divergence-Convergence

This framework separates idea generation (divergence) from idea evaluation (convergence), preventing premature judgment.

### Framework

- **Divergence Phase**: Generate many ideas without evaluation (suspend judgment)
- **Convergence Phase**: Evaluate, filter, and combine ideas
- Cycle through phases for iterative refinement

### Best For

Encouraging quantity and novelty, preventing groupthink, structured ideation.

### Execution Pattern

Generate 5-6 ideas rapidly without evaluation. Then apply evaluation criteria to assess.

## 6. Forced Connections

Forced Connections (also called Attribute Listing) combines unrelated concepts to generate novel ideas.

### Framework

- List key attributes of the problem/product
- Identify unrelated concepts or products
- Systematically combine attributes with external concepts
- Explore implications of the combinations

### Best For

Cross-disciplinary innovation, product mashups, unique market positioning.

### Execution Pattern

List 3-4 key attributes of the core topic. Combine each with 2-3 external concepts to generate ideas.

## Implementation Notes

When using these frameworks in the brainstorming-partner skill:

1. **Adaptability**: Each framework is applied flexibly to the specific brainstorming context
1. **Combination**: Multiple frameworks generate diverse perspectives on the same problem
1. **Idea Diversity**: Different frameworks naturally produce different types of ideas
1. **Framework Selection**: All frameworks are applied systematically for comprehensive coverage
1. **Prompt Engineering**: Clear prompts guide the AI to follow each framework's logic precisely
